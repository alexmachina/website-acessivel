Five man Football

Football for the visually impaired, also known as futsal and football for five, is an adaptation of football for visually impaired athletes, including the blind. The sport, governed by the International Federation of Sports for the Blind (IBSA), is played with modified FIFA rules. The playing field is smaller and surrounded by boards. Each team has five players, including the goalkeeper. Teams can still have a guide, which is positioned off the playing field, to watch the players driving them. The ball is equipped with a device that makes noise for players to locate it by sound. The games have two parts of 25 minutes each, with an interval of 10 minutes. B1 - Total or almost totally blind athletes; From the perception of light to the perception of light, but from the impossibility of recognizing the shape of a hand. B2 - Athletes with partial vision; Can recognize the shape of a hand with 2/60 visual acuity or a visual field of less than 5 degrees. B3 - Athletes with partial vision; Visual acuity of 2/60 to 6/60 or visual fields of 5 to 20 degrees. Teams may use vision athletes such as Goalkeepers / Goalkeepers and Guards; Goalkeepers / Goalkeepers may not have been registered with FIFA for a maximum of five years. There are two types of competition. For Class B1 games, only B1 class athletes are permitted, with the exception of goalkeepers and guides, who may be of classes B2, B3 or who see. For Class B2 / B3 games, teams can line up with players of classes B2 and B3; At least two B2 class players must be on the field at the same time. Football of five in Europe was developed in Spain.
